# Office Delivery Checklist

## Content

- Confirm required sections, sheets, slides, pages, attachments, and requested output formats.
- Verify names, dates, figures, units, terminology, citations, and source periods.
- Remove placeholders, TODO text, sample values, hidden notes, and unintended personal data.
- Distinguish facts, assumptions, forecasts, and recommendations.

## Fidelity and function

- Confirm the output opens in the intended application without repair warnings.
- Confirm formulas, links, fields, media, tables, and navigation work as required.
- Compare target content and structure against the source or template.
- Confirm originals remain unchanged unless overwrite was explicitly requested.

## Visual and print quality

- Inspect user-facing pages, sheets, and slides at realistic zoom or projection size.
- Check clipping, overflow, font substitution, image quality, spacing, alignment, contrast, and reading order.
- Inspect print preview or PDF for print-oriented artifacts.
- Check representative edge cases and every page/slide for short high-value deliverables.

## Compatibility and accessibility

- Check target Office version, locale, fonts, macros, links, and external dependencies.
- Ensure color is not the only carrier of meaning.
- Check language metadata, alternative descriptions, reading order, and selectable text where applicable.
- State accessibility or compatibility aspects that were not verified.

## Packaging

- Use descriptive filenames with the correct extension and version/date convention when helpful.
- Deliver only the requested source and final formats.
- Remove temporary images, XML, logs, lock files, and unpacked directories.
- Summarize outputs and any material limitations concisely.
